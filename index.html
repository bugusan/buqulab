Our Mission

BuQuLab (Bugu Quantum Research Laboratory) is engineering a fundamental revolution in the quantum computing stack. Founded by researchers from North Carolina State University, we specialize in the development of superconducting transmon qubits with a primary focus on overcoming the critical bottlenecks of the NISQ era: Readout, Reset, and Error Correction.

Hybrid Innovation Track

We develop both Hardware-Agnostic Control Systems and Proprietary Hardware Topologies. Our research focuses on:

  • Active Leakage Suppression: Time-gated protocols to mitigate non-computational state transitions.
  • Revolutionary Reset Mechanisms: Fast, high-fidelity qubit reset mechanisms to reduce duty cycles.
  • Next-Gen Architecture: Designing superconducting qubit topologies that natively integrate with error-suppression protocols.

Cloud-Native Research Ecosystem

Our development pipeline is powered by Amazon Braket. We utilize the Rigetti Ankaa-3 QPU for hardware benchmarking and the SV1 Vector Simulator for high-qubit-count verification of our error-correction mechanisms.
